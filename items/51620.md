## Summary

Keep open. Current main has useful restart hardening, including restart deferral, in-process lane reset, user-visible gateway-draining replies, and durable restart-continuation delivery for the restart sentinel. But it still does not implement the issue's central requested behavior: durable persistence and replay of arbitrary pending followup queue items or drain-window inbound messages across a real gateway process restart. The two linked PRs (#51623 and #51803) are open, unmerged, and explicitly only Phase 1 attempts, leaving drain-window arrivals and channel ACK deferral unresolved.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep #51620 open as the broad reliability tracker. The best product/code path is to adapt or supersede the Phase 1 PR work into a current-main design that durably serializes followup queue entries before shutdown, replays them through normal dispatch after startup with idempotency/TTL safeguards, then adds a separate drain-window capture and per-channel ACK/redelivery contract for transports such as Telegram webhook and Slack Socket Mode.

## Risks / Open Questions

- Closing this now would conflate restart-sentinel continuation recovery with general inbound-message durability; they solve different queues.
- Any eventual fix should avoid replaying stale or duplicate messages and should define per-channel ACK/redelivery semantics, especially for webhook and socket transports.
