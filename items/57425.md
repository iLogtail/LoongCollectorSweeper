## Summary

Current main contains meaningful partial restart-hardening work, including active-work drain deferral, queued-lane preservation after in-process restarts, targeted main-session recovery, and targeted subagent orphan recovery. It does not fully implement #57425's broader request: no restart readiness gate/force flow, no pre-restart active-session/cron manifest, drain-window enqueues still fail fast rather than being durably replayed, and interrupted cron runs are still marked failed rather than retried. A maintainer comment on the issue also explicitly says PR #71465 only covers the active-run kill portion and leaves the broader session recovery/interruption-propagation scope open.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep #57425 open or convert it into a tracked umbrella for the remaining restart-recovery scope: durable pre-restart/interruption manifest, post-start recovery for unanswered channel sessions and parent/subagent notification, configurable cron retry of interrupted runs, drain-window message persistence/replay, and a user/agent-visible restart readiness gate with an explicit force path. The already-landed #71465 drain work and current targeted session/subagent recovery should be treated as partial foundations, not as completing the issue.
