## Summary

Keep open. Current `main` does not implement Telegram Business Chat handling end-to-end, and the PR still has unresolved correctness concerns from review. This is useful work on a real gap, not something that can be conservatively auto-closed.

## Evidence

- **Current main only wires inbound Telegram messages through `message` and `channel_post` handlers:** `registerTelegramHandlers` routes inbound traffic into `handleInboundMessageLike(...)` from `bot.on("message")` and `bot.on("channel_post")`. There is no corresponding `bot.on("business_message")` or `bot.on("business_connection")` handler in current `main`. That means Telegram Business updates are not processed by the existing inbound pipeline today.
  - file: [extensions/telegram/src/bot-handlers.runtime.ts:1940](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/c070509b7f8fdbe479e5d829643782c272e30e83/extensions/telegram/src/bot-handlers.runtime.ts#L1940)
  - command: `nl -ba extensions/telegram/src/bot-handlers.runtime.ts | sed -n '1848,2018p'`
  - sha: [c070509b7f8f](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/commit/c070509b7f8fdbe479e5d829643782c272e30e83)
- **Current dedupe/update-key contract omits business update fields:** `TelegramUpdateKeyContext` on `main` includes `message`, `edited_message`, `channel_post`, `edited_channel_post`, and callback data, but no `business_message`, `edited_business_message`, or business connection identifier fields. The current dedupe key builder therefore does not cover the business update shapes proposed in the PR.
  - file: [extensions/telegram/src/bot-updates.ts:17](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/c070509b7f8fdbe479e5d829643782c272e30e83/extensions/telegram/src/bot-updates.ts#L17)
  - command: `nl -ba extensions/telegram/src/bot-updates.ts | sed -n '1,120p'`

- **Allowed updates include business update types, but main lacks matching handling:** `allowed-updates.ts` already includes `business_connection`, `business_message`, `edited_business_message`, and `deleted_business_messages` in the fallback/default update type lists. So `main` is already prepared to request those Telegram update types, but the rest of the Telegram extension does not yet consume them.
  - file: [extensions/telegram/src/allowed-updates.ts:8](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/c070509b7f8fdbe479e5d829643782c272e30e83/extensions/telegram/src/allowed-updates.ts#L8)

That upstream contract means OpenClaw cannot rely on its existing `bot.on("message")` handler to pick up `business_message` updates automatically.
  - command: `TARBALL=$(npm view grammy@1.42.0 dist.tarball); curl -Lsf "$TARBALL" | tar -xzOf - package/out/context.d.ts | rg -n "businessConnection|businessMessage" -S; curl -Lsf "$TARBALL" | tar -xzOf - package/out/filter.js | sed -n '368,404p'`

Those are reviewable fixups, not evidence that the PR is obsolete or should be closed by cleanup automation.
